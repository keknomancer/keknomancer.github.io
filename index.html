In the 1970s, if you happened to catch the eye of a big tech corp, they would literally buy you a house and a car and a ticket to whatever place the company operated out of to have you. Now, you have to bust your ass just to get an interview for a job that pays a quarter of what it did then, with 10x the responsibilities because the offices that account for 75% of the business' overhead are too inefficient to actually do what they're supposed to. Additionally, if you do have to move for the job, that's on you. Develop a chronic illness as a result of busting your ass for years? You get replaced immediately. Supervisor won't do his job? Now you do his job too. And what do you get after 5 years of that? Another $5k a year. After 30? You're lucky if you get a pension. Now everything is 2x as expensive as it was a few years ago, and inflation is more than the laughable pay increases you get. They make the rules, so don't play the game. THE HOUSE ALWAYS WINS.
